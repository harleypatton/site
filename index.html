Projects

Exclusive Quark and Gluon Jet Properties

I developed a method for extracting exclusive quark and gluon probablity distributions from mixed particle jets resulting from proton-proton collisons measured at the ATLAS particle detector at CERN.

First, jets are built from topological calorimeter clusters using a cluster weighting (LCW) algorithm. We cannot directly observe quark and gluon jets (we always get a mixture). However, by exploiting variations in the quark/gluon fraction with respect to transverse momentum, I was able to extract exclusive quark and gluon distributions from the data.

I demonstrated the effectiveness of this method on particle-level data simulated in Pythia 8 (particle simulator) by the ATLAS Monte Carlo. This data was simulated with a center of mass energy of 13 TeV to be consistent with the Run 2 data from the Large Hadron Collider restart in 2016. After running my code against millions of simulated collsion events using NERSC (National Energy Research Scientific Computing Center) supercomputers, I found my model to have excellent closure over momenta in the range of 500 - 3000 GeV.

I gave a talk on this project at the ATLAS annual meeting at Lawrence Berkeley National Lab in January of 2017. Sildes avaliable here.
