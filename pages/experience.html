I'm part of Lawrence Berkeley National Lab's ATLAS Analysis Team, which works with data brought in from the ATLAS Experiment at the Large Hadron Collider at CERN. I've worked on the following projects over the last two years:

  • Training generative adverserial networks to simulate the effects of particle collision events in a way indistinguishable from the events measured at CERN, motivated by the fact that feed-forward networks are much more efficient than the current state-of-the-art simulators.
  • Developed a heuristic that can be used to classify particles from measured energy-loss readouts. I showed that a classifier using this hueristic performs as well an artificial neural network, and proved its optimality. (arXiv)
  • Developed a method for extracting exclusive quark/gluon probablity distributions from mixed particle jets measured at CERN. (slides)
