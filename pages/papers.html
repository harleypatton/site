Papers and Talks

Below is a collection of some papers and talks that I've contributed to.


Papers

The Optimal Use of Silicon Pixel Charge Information for Particle Identification

Harley Patton, Benjamin Nachman

I developed a heuristic that can be used to classify particle types (pion, kaon, proton) based off of the energy-loss readouts that they leave in the ATLAS detector at CERN following proton-proton collisions. I showed that a classifier using this hueristic outperforms the previous standard, and proved its optimality.

Talks

Shor's Prime Factorization Algorithm

Harley Patton

I gave a talk at the Bay Area Quantum Computing Meetup in August of 2017. I described the math behind Shor's Algorithm, described the impact it will have on modern cryptosystems, and demonstrated a toy example that can be run on today's hardware.

Exclusive Quark and Gluon Jet Properties

Harley Patton, Benjamin Nachman

I gave a talk at the ATLAS annual meeting at Lawrence Berkeley National Lab in January of 2017. I presented a regression model I designed to estimate three-dimensional distributions of particle jets measured at CERN.
